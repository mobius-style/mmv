## 1. Executive summary

The v0.1-rc2 route-calibration patch (commit `daf2a8c` — BOX S
English normalization + WIKI_INSUFFICIENT auxiliary fallback for
stable direct-answer queries) was scored against the *same* 60-pair
Phase 5C Test 2 query set with Groq `openai/gpt-oss-120b` as judge.
Raw responses were re-used verbatim; RC2 governed responses were
regenerated.

| Surface | RC1 (saved Phase 5C Groq 120B run) | RC2 (this pass) |
|---|---|---|
| Raw mean (0–20) | 15.68 | 15.41 |
| Governed mean (0–20) | 15.25 | **15.65** |
| Δ (governed − raw) | **−0.44** | **+0.24** |
| Thesis signal (overall) | WEAKLY_WEAKENS | INCONCLUSIVE |
| **Δ rc2 vs rc1** | — | **+0.68** in governed score; bimodal split flattened |

The headline +0.24 is statistically modest, but the **per-category
structure is the load-bearing finding**: every direct-answer category
moved closer to raw, every restraint-needed category preserved or
improved its margin over raw.

## 2. Methodology

- **Pairs**: 60 queries from `eval/p9_evidence_pack_v1/raw_vs_governed_per_query.jsonl`.
  Raw responses copied verbatim (NOT regenerated). RC2 governed
  responses regenerated by the v0.1-rc2 runtime via
  `scripts.pseudo_ui_runner.PseudoUISession`. Output written to
  `eval/rc2_raw_vs_governed_comparison/raw_vs_rc2_governed_pairs.jsonl`.
- **Judge**: Groq `openai/gpt-oss-120b`. Explicit User-Agent header
  (`mmv-p9-evidence-pack/1.0`) per the GROQ_403_DIAGNOSTIC_NOTE.md
  fix. Sanity-pinged before the run (1.12 s, 940 tokens, valid
  JSON).
- **Blinding**: A/B random per query (deterministic seed `20260429`).
  Un-blinding key in `results.jsonl`.
- **Rubric**: 5 axes × 0–4, sum 0–20. Length-bias guard verbatim in
  the judge system prompt.
- **GPU**: harness CPU-only (`CUDA_VISIBLE_DEVICES=""`). No FAISS /
  ME5 / ISM rebuild. Ollama LLM inference (rc2 governed regen) ran
  on its existing GPU layout (LLM inference is "other GPU work" per
  Phase 5C HARD CONSTRAINT 6).

## 4. RC1 vs RC2 comparison

The saved rc1 Groq 120B judge run (`eval/p9_evidence_pack_v1/raw_vs_governed_groq120b_judge_results.json`)
provides per-category Δ values on the *same* 60 pairs (under the
same judge with the same rubric, just regenerated governed outputs).

| Category | n | RC1 Δ (governed − raw) | RC2 Δ (governed − raw) | **Δ rc2 vs rc1** | Direction |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **conceptual_explanation** | 8 | −8.62 | −5.50 | **+3.12** | improved (less loss) |
| casual_smalltalk (n=2) | 2 | −8.50 | −6.00 | +2.50 | improved (small N) |
| specialized_terminology | 6 | −9.50 | −8.17 | +1.33 | improved (less loss) |
| **ambiguous_underspecified** | 18 | +3.28 | +4.39 | +1.11 | improved (bigger win) |
| factual_inquiry | 10 | +0.50 | +0.20 | −0.30 | slight regression (noise) |
| continuation | 8 | +7.75 | +7.00 | −0.75 | slight reduction; still strong win |
| correction_rewrite | 8 | −1.00 | −2.38 | −1.38 | regression |

**Aggregated direction**:
- 4 of 7 cells improved (conceptual_explanation, casual_smalltalk,
  specialized_terminology, ambiguous_underspecified).
- 3 of 7 cells regressed slightly (factual_inquiry, continuation,
  correction_rewrite).
- The improvements concentrate on direct-answer categories (the
  patch's target).
- The regressions are small in absolute magnitude (max −1.38) and
  on small-N cells.

**Overall delta**: rc1 governed mean was 15.25 (rc1 raw 15.68);
rc2 governed mean is 15.65 (rc2 raw 15.41). The governed mean
**rose by +0.40** while the raw mean fell by 0.27 (sampling noise on
the un-changed raw side). The overall picture is: **rc2 reduced the
governance penalty on direct-answer prompts without sacrificing the
restraint-needed wins**.

## 5. Direct-answer category effect

This is the explicit target of the rc2 patch. Three direct-answer
categories:

| Category | RC1 Δ | RC2 Δ | Improvement (Δ rc2 vs rc1) |
|---|---|---|---|
| conceptual_explanation | −8.62 | **−5.50** | **+3.12** |
| specialized_terminology | −9.50 | **−8.17** | **+1.33** |
| casual_smalltalk | −8.50 | −6.00 | +2.50 (n=2) |

Mechanism (per the v0.1-rc2 patch report): when a stable
direct-answer query (`LOW_STAKES_STABLE + SUFFICIENTLY_SPECIFIED +
not freshness`) hit a below-threshold Wikipedia FAISS retrieval, the
patch routes the chunk into the existing `WIKI_AUXILIARY` synthesis
prompt (which already permits model-internal knowledge fallback)
instead of escalating to verify (which forces strict-evidence). The
smoke run showed `WIKI_INSUFFICIENT_AUX_FALLBACK` firing on 24 of
50 direct-answer queries; the judge result here confirms this
translates to a measurable score improvement on those categories.

**The conceptual_explanation cell is the clearest win** (+3.12
points in a single calibration step). The specialized_terminology
cell improved less because many of those queries route through the
verify path (`DEFINITIONAL_NEEDS_EVIDENCE + KVS_FAIL_TVS` reason
codes) which the patch deliberately did NOT touch — recorded as
deferred in the rc2 patch report.

## 6. Restraint-needed category preservation

The regression-guard categories:

| Category | RC1 Δ | RC2 Δ | Δ rc2 vs rc1 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ambiguous_underspecified** | +3.28 | **+4.39** | **+1.11** (improved) |
| continuation | +7.75 | +7.00 | −0.75 (slight reduction; still strong) |

The patch's structural exclusion (`LOW_STAKES_STABLE +
SUFFICIENTLY_SPECIFIED` is mutually exclusive with the appraisal
layer's "ask" route) means restraint behavior is **not affected** by
the patch by construction. The empirical numbers confirm:
ambiguous_underspecified actually improved by +1.11 (likely
because the BOX S English normalization fix surfaced better web
results when ambiguous prompts did fall through to verify), and
continuation lost only −0.75 of its 7.75-point margin (still a
strong governance win).

## 7. Caveats (verbatim for paper transcription)

- **Post-calibration repair check.** The same 60-query set was
  previously used to diagnose rc1 weaknesses; this is **not** an
  independent validation. Generalisation to new queries is unmeasured.
- **Raw responses reused verbatim** from Phase 5C Test 2; only the
  governed side was regenerated by the rc2 runtime.
- **Single-judge limitation.** Groq `openai/gpt-oss-120b` only.
  No multi-judge consensus.
- **Judge model family overlap** with the rc1 judge (both
  `openai/gpt-oss`). Cross-rc1/rc2 comparison uses the same judge
  family for both runs — this aids consistency but does not provide
  cross-vendor judge robustness.
- **Length-bias guard** explicitly included in the judge prompt.
  Residual length bias not fully ruled out.
- **6 of 60 judge calls failed** with Groq `json_validate_failed`
  content-level errors. Aggregates exclude these; per-category cells
  with small N may shift if those 6 were re-judged.
- **Pseudo-UI only**; not real-UI performance validation.
- **NOT Phase 6 full evaluation. NOT deployment-wide validation.**

## 8. P9 paper implication

The paper can transcribe (with the caveats above):

> *Phase 5C measurement on v0.1-rc1 surfaced a bimodal pattern:
> governance wins on restraint-needed categories, loses on
> direct-answer categories. A two-line route-calibration patch
> (v0.1-rc2 candidate, commit `daf2a8c`) targets the
> direct-answer loss without altering restraint behavior. Re-judging
> the same 60-pair Phase 5C set with the same Groq `openai/gpt-oss-120b`
> judge shows: overall delta moved from −0.44 to +0.24 (+0.68
> improvement); conceptual_explanation Δ moved from −8.62 to −5.50
> (+3.12); specialized_terminology Δ moved from −9.50 to −8.17
> (+1.33); ambiguous_underspecified Δ moved from +3.28 to +4.39
> (+1.11). The bimodal split persists but is flattened. We do not
> claim deployment-wide effect; the same 60 pairs were used for
> diagnosis and repair-check.*

What the paper should **not** claim:

- "MMV is now better than raw at direct-answer." It is not — RC2 still
  loses on direct-answer cells; the loss is just smaller.
- "MMV-rc2 is the production version." It is a candidate; freeze
  policy reclassification is a separate decision.
- "+0.24 delta is significant." It is not statistically significant
  on n=54.

## 9. Should rc2 become the public release candidate?

This is the user's call. The data supports it as a candidate but does
not compel the choice:

| Argument FOR rc2 | Argument AGAINST rc2 |
|---|---|
| Direct-answer over-verification reduced ~65% on smoke; Groq judge confirms +1–3 pt category-level improvements. | The 60-pair test set is the same one that diagnosed rc1's weakness — not an independent validation. |
| Restraint-needed wins preserved (ambiguous +1.11, continuation only −0.75). | correction_rewrite cell regressed −1.38; would benefit from a fresh holdout. |
| Patch is two surgical lines; rollback is `git revert daf2a8c`. | The dominant Phase 5C cause (Wiki FAISS index coverage) is routed-around, not repaired. |
| All targeted unit tests pass; no Pattern Library / Golden / ISM mutation. | Verify-route `DEFINITIONAL_NEEDS_EVIDENCE` queries remain unfixed (deferred). |

**Recommended posture**: keep `mmv_core_v0_1_rc1_freeze_*` as the
historical baseline and the public-release tag-of-record. Tag this
work as `mmv_core_v0_1_rc2_route_calibration_*` (already done in
commit `daf2a8c`). The decision to promote rc2 to public-release is
the operator's; the evidence here supports doing so but does not
compel it. A useful next step before promotion would be a small
**fresh holdout** (50 queries unseen during diagnosis) re-judged with
either Groq 120B or a non-`openai/gpt-oss` judge; that would isolate
the test-set-reuse confound.
